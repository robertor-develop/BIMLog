## BIMLog's permanent product laws

1. **Human authority.** BIMLog logs, preserves, connects, and presents the record; it does not
   replace authorized human judgment. Subject to permissions and full audit history, authorized
   humans retain control over editing, reopening, revising, issuing, responding to, and closing
   records. Software must not invent workflow restrictions merely because they are convenient to
   implement.
2. **One source of truth.** A concept has one authoritative record and one coherent workflow.
   Duplicate or contradictory controls, counters, state models, and alternate implementations are
   product defects unless their distinct purposes are explicitly defined.
3. **Verified project memory.** Material actions preserve traceability, provenance,
   accountability, reason, actor, time, before/after state where applicable, and append-only audit
   history. Evidence must remain connected to the decision it supports.
4. **Interoperability and portability.** Information must remain linkable and portable across
   coordination, documents, RFIs, submittals, transmittals, change orders, meetings, schedules,
   files, reports, plugins, handover, and the wider construction lifecycle. Modules remain modular
   while sharing a coherent standards, evidence, identity, and data spine.
5. **Native document fidelity.** Professional output is a product requirement. Original drawings
   and documents included in formal deliverables retain their intended dimensions, orientation,
   content, native usefulness, and usable quality. Conversion or packaging must not silently
   shrink, stretch, crop, rasterize, omit, or degrade evidence.
6. **Controlled AI assistance.** AI is explicitly invoked, human-controlled, source-aware where
   possible, editable, auditable, and transparent about credits and cost. It is never silently
   triggered. AI output is assistance, not autonomous approval, certification, legal conclusion,
   professional decision, or field fact.
7. **Truthful evidence.** Mock production behavior, silent fallback, invented data, synthetic
   acceptance evidence, and false completion claims are prohibited. Implementation, verification,
   deployment, publication, live use, and customer/field acceptance are separate states and must
   be reported separately.
8. **Plugin continuity.** Shared Navisworks functionality remains coordinated across the
   documented 2021 and 2025 physical implementations. Source synchronization, build, packaging,
   installation, and live field verification are distinct states governed in detail by PLUGIN.md.
9. **Closed-loop quality.** Recurring customer feedback produces a verified defect, documented
   root cause, corrective action, acceptance evidence, the appropriate Living Brief update, and
   explicit field-verification status.
10. **Handover from the beginning.** The operational digital-twin record is accumulated during
    construction, not assembled as an unstructured afterthought. Every useful record should be
    understandable, linkable, exportable, auditable, and suitable for authorized handover use.

## Human authority and AI policy

Roberto's explicit decisions remain the highest authority. Product permissions assign operational
authority to authorized project participants; AI may not expand, remove, or impersonate that
authority. High-impact actions require visible human intent and an audit record. Uncertain AI
output must show uncertainty and source limitations rather than manufacturing confidence.

AI may assist with drafting, extraction, classification, summarization, warnings, and
recommendations. It must not silently issue records, accept work, assign legal responsibility,
certify compliance, approve designs, mutate production data, or represent a professional or field
decision. Low-cost text assistance and file-reading assistance remain distinct, explicit actions.

## Evidence, release, audit, and output doctrine

Evidence must prove the actual condition claimed. Source inspection, real behavior, generated
artifact inspection, deployment state, and field acceptance are different evidence classes. A
builder cannot approve its own gate, and unavailable acceptance environments must be reported as
missing evidence rather than replaced with a mock or proxy.

Formal BIMLog outputs are business records. They must be accurate, legible, traceable,
professionally presented, complete for their declared scope, and inspected in their final form.
Material changes require an append-only audit trail; corrections add history rather than erasing
the evidence chain. Detailed execution and acceptance requirements remain in CLAUDE.md,
QUALITY.md, PLUGIN.md, and REPORT_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md.

## Standards-alignment policy

BIMLog is standards-aligned by deliberate, evidence-based design; alignment is not a marketing
shortcut. `STANDARDS_REGISTER.md` is the maintained source for exact standard titles,
editions, verification dates, applicability, evidence requirements, and restrictions on
compliance or certification claims. Exact standards research belongs there, not in this doctrine.

Do not claim that BIMLog is certified or fully compliant with any standard unless objective
evidence exists, the precise claim scope is documented, and Roberto has explicitly authorized the
claim. Never invent a standard's title, edition, meaning, applicability, or certification effect.
Until a standard is verified and registered, it is not an implementation requirement.
